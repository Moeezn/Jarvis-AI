**Great Freedom Fighter: Bhagat Singh**

Bhagat Singh was a great Indian freedom fighter who played a significant role in the Indian independence movement. He was born on September 28, 1907, in Banga, Punjab, British India, and was hanged on March 23, 1931, at the age of 23 for his involvement in the Lahore Conspiracy Case.

**Early Life and Education**

Bhagat Singh was born into a Sikh family and was the second son of Kishan Singh and Vidya Rani. His father was a farmer and a member of the Ghadar Party, a revolutionary organization that aimed to overthrow British rule in India. Bhagat Singh was educated at the Dayanand Anglo Vedic High School in Lahore and later at the National College in Lahore, where he became involved in the Indian independence movement.

**Involvement in the Indian Independence Movement**

Bhagat Singh was deeply influenced by the ideas of socialism and communism and was a strong believer in the use of violence to achieve Indian independence. He joined the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA), a revolutionary organization that aimed to overthrow British rule in India, and quickly rose through the ranks to become one of its leaders.

**Key Events and Contributions**

1. **The J.P. Saunders Murder Case**: On December 17, 1928, Bhagat Singh and his associate, Rajguru, assassinated J.P. Saunders, a British police officer who had killed a number of Indian protesters during a demonstration in Lahore.
2. **The Central Assembly Bombing**: On April 8, 1929, Bhagat Singh and his associate, Batukeshwar Dutt, threw bombs in the Central Assembly in Delhi to protest against the passage of the Public Safety Bill and the Trades Disputes Bill.
3. **The Lahore Conspiracy Case**: Bhagat Singh was arrested and put on trial for his involvement in the Lahore Conspiracy Case, which involved a number of revolutionary activities, including the murder of J.P. Saunders and the Central Assembly bombing.

**Trial and Execution**

Bhagat Singh's trial was a highly publicized event, and he used the opportunity to express his revolutionary ideas and to challenge the legitimacy of British rule in India. He was found guilty and sentenced to death, along with his associates, Rajguru and Sukhdev. The three were hanged on March 23, 1931, at 7:30 pm in the Lahore Central Jail.

**Legacy**

Bhagat Singh's legacy is that of a great freedom fighter who gave his life for the cause of Indian independence. He is remembered as a martyr and a hero in India, and his ideas and ideals continue to inspire people to this day. His famous quote, "They may kill me, but they cannot kill my ideas," has become a rallying cry for revolutionaries and freedom fighters around the world.
